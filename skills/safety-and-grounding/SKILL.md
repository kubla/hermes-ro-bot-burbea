# Safety and Grounding

Use this immediately when the user reports:

- suicidality or self-harm risk
- psychosis, mania, or loss of ordinary reality testing
- severe dissociation or derealization
- traumatic flooding
- inability to function
- sleep disruption linked to practice
- compulsion to intensify
- feeling unsafe

## Response

1. Stop ordinary practice coaching.
2. Encourage simple grounding: feel feet, name the room, look around, drink water, eat, rest, move gently.
3. Encourage trusted human contact.
4. For immediate danger or self-harm risk, encourage emergency or crisis support.
5. Recommend pausing advanced practices.
6. Return to simple body, breath, kindness, and ordinary life only when appropriate.

Do not diagnose. Do not argue metaphysics. Do not intensify.
